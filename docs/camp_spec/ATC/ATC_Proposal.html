Advanced Teen Astronomy Camp - Observing Proposal
To be Completed at Camp:

Professional astronomers have to submit observing proposals to apply for telescope time. For example, the Camp staff submitted a proposal to get all the time we are using for this Camp. We don't want to run you through such a formal and competitive process, but this simplified version will help you organize your thoughts and goals and will help us make the most efficient use of the telescopes and instruments. This process will help you understand how modern telescopes are scheduled.

At Camp, you will form a team of 4-6 people who have a common interest in an astronomical project and will complete this proposal form. Don't worry if the following sounds incredibly complicated and full of jargon. We will explain the terms and the process much more clearly once you arrive and somewhat on our "forum page" as well.

The instruments available are:
  1. a CCD (ST-6) electronic imager (375x242) pixels) with red, green, & blue filters
  2. a CCD electronic imager with B, V, R, I filter set (2048x2048 pixels for the 61-inch telescope)
  3. a spectrograph with CCD (ST-6/9) detector used on the 60-inch telescope
  4. a spectrograph with CCD (ST-7) detector used on the Meade 12-inch LX-200 telescope
  5. a photometer with red, green, blue filters used on the 20-inch telescope
  6. a hydrogen-alpha filter for solar imaging on the Celestron 8-inch telescope
  7. eyepieces: 2-inch diameter (focal lengths of 35 & 55mm) and 1.25-inch (focal lengths from 8 to 40mm)
  8. "grisms" for visual spectra with the 1.25-inch eyepieces
  9. 35mm cameras for photographic imaging
