The Liquid Nitrogen Cannon

In a typical launch, the explosive force of a plastic "pop" bottle, rupturing under pressure from evaporating liquid nitrogen, sends a 2 liter container of water ~400 feet into the air.

Such experiments help demonstrate various principles of physics, engineering, and chemistry, including pressure, evaporation, Newton's laws of motion, ballistic motion, etc. They also provide opportunities to predict and measure force, altitude, time aloft, velocity, and acceleration.

Liquid nitrogen experiments have long been an exciting "lecture" at Astronomy Camp. Here students become engineers and consider the design of a spaceship for traveling to the cold temperatures (-320 F) at Saturn.
